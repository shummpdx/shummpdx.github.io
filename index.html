Experience

I currently volunteer with the Computer Action Team (CAT) at PSU where I am a mentor, technical writer, and provide technical support to students at the Mashee College of Engineering and Computer Science (MCECS). In the fall, I look forward to getting the oppertunity to work in the networking closets as a wiring minon setting up and maintaining hosts at MCECS.

My first year with the CAT was spent working the help desk responding to tickets and ensuring the computer labs were functioning properly. On Friday evenings, we would attend weekly lectures about not only the infrastructure within MCECS, but also, user services, platform specific user/admin roles (Windows/Linux), and networking concepts.

The following summer I took on the role of a mentor where I updated many of the presentations that would be given to new volunteers to help through the onboarding process. I created homework assignments and speaking notes. In the fall I led 8 2-hour virtual orientation sessions with a group of 6 volunteers ensuring that they felt comfortable navigating the many tools and resoucers we offer.

Once in-person classes resume, I will begin to work within the networking closest maintaining and configuring hosts within the MCECS network. I look forward to learning a lot and, eventually, passing on the skills and concepts I have learned.
